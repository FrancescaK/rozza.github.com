Day 24 - Monday, Monday

Jan 25th, 2011

Mondays come round all to quickly but luckily they seem to go quickly too!

I’m still really enjoying the new regime of cycling to work and have been quite lucky with the weather.  Last night it was wet and I noticed the Boris bike wasn’t too grippy on the cobbles, so took extra care but I still managed an Avg pace of under 7 minutes miles!  So since starting a couple of weeks ago my pace has improved by a minute a mile!  Thats including being caught up by all the traffic lights last night as Murphys Law was in action and I wanted to catch an earlier train, I missed it by a minute :(

I was planning on going for a swim once I got home from work, but feeling a little run down I decided to skip it and rest up.

 

Stats (Bike Totals):

Distance: 3.41 mi
Duration: 00:23:46
Avg. Pace: 6:58min/mi

Day 23 - Muddy and Momentous

Jan 23rd, 2011

Up with the larks this morning to go for my weekly run with Adam. Still very dark in the mornings, so the first off road section was slightly hazardous.  It seems to have got so much muddier in the last two weeks the fields have changed into a quagmire of mud and puddles. So not too dissimilar from last weekend!

The run felt great, in fact managed negative splits.  By the end my legs were so tired I decided a cold bath would help them.   Now for my momentous moment of the day: I managed a cold bath without screaming like a girl!  As my legs were so muddy, I didn’t stretch out after the run big mistake as after the cold bath my legs felt too cold to stretch out…

Stats:

Distance: 8:02 mi
Duration: 01:26:28
Avg. Pace: 10:47min/mi

Day 22 - Gym Will Fix It

Jan 22nd, 2011

Well today I went to the gym for 45 minutes.  The plan for the gym is to focus on upper body and core strength, I figure the running is good for the legs and cycling will be to!  So after beasting myself on the weights (doing relatively small weights on the various torture machines) my arms, chest and back are now tender (read f’ing hurt).  So much so I’m really glad I have power steering, as I could really feel it straight after on the drive home!  I’m hoping this is a newbie thing and that I will soon get used to doing weights and be able to lift weights that someone my size should be able to!
