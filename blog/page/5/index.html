Day 20 - Commute and Gym

Jan 21st, 2011

Finally, I can use the gym, I’m paying for!  After signing up and paying on MondayI was told I had to do an induction before being allowed to use the gym.  Well, I went last night and after being wieghed, measured; height, blood pressure and pulse: 14st 10lb, 193cm, 83/130, 48 bpm.  I told them I had used the gym before and after being tested I could use a machine, they said I was free to use the gym! So I spent 30 mins doing some weights and finished off with a 5 minute row.

If anyone has any good advice on resources for planning a core strength workout - let me know!  Its my main area of focus for the gym in prep for my first Olympic triathlon in July!

The commute was good, did my fastest time to kings cross in the evening!  However, I did see on cyclist take out a young lady who stepped out into the cycling lane at a traffic lights without looking - note to self cycle defensively!

Day 18 - Double Commute

Jan 19th, 2011

I guess the grim hurt more than I realised, I had a disturbed nights sleep as both kids decided to wake me up at some point during the night.  I then couldn’t go back to sleep and my legs were so achey I had to take some Ibuprofen!  I’m over doing it, so I decided the 18th would be a rest day, which in reality meant Boris Biking it to and from work.

I still love the Boris bikes and I’ve traded 30 mins of daily tube crush for under 30 mins of exhilarating pedalling.   I’m quite chilled by nature but I’m wary of cars and vans, so I accept they will not notice me, they will cut me up and / or try and run me over; so I cycle defensively.  However, occasionally find myself wanting to overtake all cyclists in front of me - is that normal?

Day 16 - the Grim

Jan 16th, 2011

After a week of not running to rest my knee, the day of the grim had arrived and I loved it.

The course was good, lots of puddles and lots you can avoid initially, but wheres the fun in that?  The sooner your feet get wet the quicker you get used to it.  At around 2 miles you couldn’t really avoid the puddles - up to about thigh height at this point.  Cold toes dont make for fun running, but you soon get them warmed up and then the later puddles feel refreshing!  Some were strangley warm, probably best not to think about that…

The course was good and around 5 miles there was a real stinker of an obstacle; a cargo net and some excellent mud.  Then into some water, I was in a rut and the water was up to my waist; being 6’ 2” I can confirm it was deep!!  Everyone slowed down to tackle that one, so once out I speeded up, but with tired legs and mud on my glasses I tripped and went flying.  Everyone running by checked I was ok and one chap even gave me a score: 8.5 - Not Bad!

At 6 miles I thought only 2 and then in!!  So I speeded up and OMG the last mile - b*stard.  I liked the big dipper hill and finally nearing the end there was some more water, I stepped over a tree trunk and one leg disappeared to my thigh!  Once I struggled out of that, my legs felt like blocks of lead, but it was the on-in.  Into the big final puddle too fast I nearly went flying, out and round the corner and done!

A great event, tough on the legs but a hell of a lot of fun!  I’ll be back next year for sure.
